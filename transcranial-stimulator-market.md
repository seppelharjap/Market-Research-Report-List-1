<p><ul><li>Transcranial Electrical Stimulators</li><li>Transcranial Magnetic Stimulators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The transcranial stimulator market encompasses two main types: Transcranial Electrical Stimulators (TES) and Transcranial Magnetic Stimulators (TMS). TES uses direct or alternating electrical currents to modulate neuronal activity, primarily for therapeutic and research purposes. In contrast, TMS employs magnetic fields to induce electrical currents in specific brain regions, offering non-invasive treatment options for conditions like depression and anxiety. Both technologies are vital for neurostimulation, cognitive enhancement, and neurological research, each exhibiting unique mechanisms and applications.</p></p>
