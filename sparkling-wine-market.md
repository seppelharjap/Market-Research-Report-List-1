<p><p>The sparkling wine market can be divided into two main types: normal sparkling wine and semi-sparkling wine. Normal sparkling wine features a consistent effervescence due to secondary fermentation, producing a high level of bubbles, while examples include Champagne and Prosecco. In contrast, semi-sparkling wine has a lower carbonation level, resulting in a gentler fizz, like frizzante wines. Both categories appeal to diverse consumer preferences, contributing to their distinct positions in the global wine market.</p></p>
