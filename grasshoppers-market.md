<p><p>Grasshoppers are categorized into two main market types: long-horned and short-horned grasshoppers. Long-horned grasshoppers, characterized by their elongated antennae, often inhabit moist and grassy environments. They are typically found in more humid regions and can be crucial for local ecosystems. Short-horned grasshoppers, with their shorter antennae, thrive in drier habitats and are more commonly found in open fields and deserts. Both types play essential roles in agriculture and biodiversity, influencing crop production and natural pest control dynamics.</p></p>
