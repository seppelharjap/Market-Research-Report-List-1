<p><p>PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) vascular grafts are medical devices used in vascular surgery to replace or bypass damaged blood vessels. These grafts are made from a biocompatible material that is resistant to thrombosis and has a low friction coefficient, making them suitable for long-term implantation. PTFE grafts are primarily utilized in procedures related to peripheral artery disease, coronary artery bypass, and dialysis access.</p><p>The PTFE vascular grafts market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in surgical techniques, and a growing aging population. Rising awareness about minimally invasive surgical procedures and technological innovations in graft manufacturing further enhance market dynamics. </p><p>Recent trends include the development of grafts with improved durability and biocompatibility, as well as the integration of antimicrobial properties to reduce infection rates. <p><p>PTFE vascular grafts are categorized into two main types based on diameter: large diameter and small diameter grafts. Large diameter grafts, typically above 6 mm, are primarily used in arterial bypass procedures, benefiting from higher blood flow capacity. In contrast, small diameter grafts, usually below 6 mm, are essential for more intricate applications such as bypass surgeries in smaller arteries or vascular access for dialysis. The selection between these types depends on the specific clinical needs and the anatomical context of the patient.</p></p>
