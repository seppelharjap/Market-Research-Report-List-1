<p><p>Surface roughness testers are categorized into two main market types: contact and non-contact methods. Contact testers involve physical probes that make direct contact with the surface, measuring roughness through deflections and vibrations, providing precise readings. Non-contact testers utilize technologies like laser triangulation or white light interferometry, capturing surface profiles without physical interaction, making them ideal for delicate materials. Each type serves specific applications across industries, ensuring accurate assessment of surface quality for improved product performance and durability.</p></p>
